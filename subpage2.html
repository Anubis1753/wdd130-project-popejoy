Why would I want Bees?

Keeping bees, even just one managed hive, offers significant environmental benefits. Bees are essential pollinators for countless plants, including many vital food crops and native wildflowers. By hosting a colony, you directly help stabilize local ecosystems, increase the yield of surrounding gardens and farms, and combat the alarming decline of global bee populations. How is your Honey Made?

Honey production starts when foraging bees collect nectar from flowers using their long, tube-like tongues. They store this nectar in a special "honey stomach" where enzymes begin to break down its complex sugars. Back at the hive, they pass the partially digested nectar to house bees, who repeatedly ingest and regurgitate it to further reduce moisture and convert the sucrose into simpler glucose and fructose. Finally, the bees deposit the mixture into honeycomb cells and fan it with their wings to evaporate the remaining water, creating thick, stable honey. Once cured, they cap the cell with beeswax to seal it for storage.
